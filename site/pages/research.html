Research

Research

My research has two lines: (1) the politicization on social media and digital platforms; and (2) the organizing dynamics of online communities in the digital ecosystem. They are often intersected with eacho other, such as membership overlap between political and non-political communities can fuel politicization, and international platform migration can be a way of alternative political expression. I have used computational and AI-assisted methods, survey, content analysis, and interviews to study online communities on various platforms like Reddit, RedNote, TikTok, etc. My work has been published in journals including New Media & Society.

Yibin Fan*, Benjamin Mako Hill, & Patricia Moy

Minor R&R

The Politics of the Non-Political: A Conceptual Review (2004-2024)

Yufan Guo & Yibin Fan*

This review traces how theorists and empirical researchers in political science and communication have conceptualized what's viewed non-political. It reports two distinct conceptual understandings of the non-political, one is institutional and the other is critical, and they each inform different conceptualization approaches in empirical studies and report different relationships between the political and the non-political.

Major R&R

Motivations and the Political Correlates of Platform Migration in International Contexts: The Case of TikTok Refugees

Yibin Fan* & Wang-Hai Tucker

This study conceptualizes international platform migration as a way of alternative political expression. By surveying platform migrants from TikTok to RedNote during 2025 "TikTok Refugees" crisis, it reports cultural openness and political expression are two most salient motivations for migration, and migration is correlated with lower of political trust, media trust, internal political efficacy and lower level of external political efficacy.

Journal of Information Technology & Politics

Major R&R

Political Talk in Non-Political Digital Spaces: A Conceptual Review

Yibin Fan*

This study reviews studies on political talk in non-political digital spaces from three epistemologies: post-positivism, interpretivism, and critical theory. It reports key concepts used to study this rising group of phenomena, and calls for a reconcillation of three distinct epistemologies in future research.

Research

Research Agenda

Politicization on social media and digital platforms

I study political communication in non-political digital contexts, such as incidental political discussion and community-level politicization.

politicization non-political incidental discussion alternative political expression

Online community dynamics in the digital ecosystem

I study ecological interactions among populations of online communities, such as how digital groups compete or cooperate with each other and platform migration.

community ecology mutualism competition platform migration multilevel ecosystem

Dissertation

Everything to do with Politics: Examining Politicization in Online Communities

This dissertation develops a framework for understanding politicization as a communicative process that unfolds through online communities and everyday digital interaction.

Recognitions: Barbara Warnick Dissertation Proposal Award Fritz International Studies Fellowship Humane Studies Fellowship

Peer-reviewed Journal Article

Unintended politics

Published at New Media & Society, this article shows political discourse in non-political digital contexts is more likely to contain policy opinion expression than in explicitly political contexts.

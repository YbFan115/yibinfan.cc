# Academic Job Search Content Checklist

## Must Have

- Name and current affiliation
- One-sentence research identity
- Research areas
- CV PDF
- Publications and working papers
- Teaching areas
- Contact email

## Strongly Recommended

- Job-market paper or featured project
- Research statement PDF
- Teaching statement PDF
- Diversity, mentoring, or service statement if needed
- Links to Google Scholar, ORCID, GitHub, and institutional page
- Downloadable syllabi or course descriptions

## Homepage Copy Formula

Use this pattern for the opening identity:

> I am a [role] studying [topic/domain] using [methods/data] to understand [larger contribution].

Examples of fields to fill in:

- role: doctoral candidate, postdoctoral researcher, assistant professor candidate
- topic/domain: political communication, computational social science, public opinion, digital platforms
- methods/data: causal inference, machine learning, surveys, text analysis, experiments
- contribution: how institutions, media, and publics respond to social change

## Search Committee Scanning Test

Within 20 seconds, a visitor should know:

- What kind of scholar you are.
- What your research is about.
- Whether you are on the market.
- Where to find your CV.
- How to contact you.

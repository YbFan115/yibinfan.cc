# Research Content

## Research Agenda

My research asks how digital communities become political, how political expression circulates through spaces not designed for formal politics, and how online groups shape public communication. Across this work, I treat politicization as a social process: everyday platforms, communities, conflicts, and migrations can turn ordinary interaction into political meaning.

Empirically, my work draws on online communities, social media discourse, platform migration cases, and comparative digital group structures. Conceptually, it connects political communication, computational social science, and community ecology to explain how digital groups shape civic and political life.

## Featured Work

### Everything to do with Politics: Examining Politicization in Online Communities

- Status: Dissertation project
- Committee: Benjamin Mako Hill (chair), Patricia Moy, Yuan Hsiao, Christopher Adolph
- Expected completion: Spring 2027

This project develops a research agenda around politicization in online communities, asking how political meaning emerges in community spaces where politics may be incidental, contested, or newly activated.

### Unintended politics: Opinion expression and incivility in incidental political discussion

- Status: Published
- Venue: New Media & Society
- Coauthors: Benjamin Mako Hill and Patricia Moy
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1177/14614448261441875

This article examines opinion expression and incivility in incidental political discussion, contributing to research on how political communication emerges outside explicitly political contexts.
